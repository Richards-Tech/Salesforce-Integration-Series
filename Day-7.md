## 🏢 Scenario

An e-commerce company uses **Salesforce**, **SAP**, and an **external ERP**.

### 📋 Business Requirements:
* ✅ Create a **Lead** in Salesforce as soon as a customer submits an enquiry.
* ✅ Synchronize **Invoices** from the ERP every night.
* ✅ Create a **Sales Order** in SAP automatically when an Opportunity is marked **Closed Won**.

---

## 🤔 Which Integration Approach Would You Choose?

* **A:** Everything should be Real-Time
* **B:** Everything should be Batch
* **C:** A combination of Real-Time, Batch & Event-Driven

---

## 💼 Real Project Insight

This isn't just a theoretical question.

I've worked on projects where a single business process used multiple integration patterns because every step had different business requirements.

> **Key takeaway:** Choosing the right pattern for each requirement makes the integration more scalable, reliable, and significantly easier to maintain.

---

## 🧠 Architect's Tip

One of the biggest mistakes developers make is asking:  
❌ *"Which integration technology should I use?"*

A better question is:  
✅ ***"What does the business actually need?"***

*The technology choice should always follow the business requirement.*

---

## 📖 Integration Term of the Day

> **Integration Pattern**  
> A proven structural approach for exchanging data between systems based on specific business needs—such as Real-Time, Batch, or Event-Driven patterns.
